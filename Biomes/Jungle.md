# Jungle

The Jungle is a dense, humid, layered ecosystem of towering trees, hanging vines, and tangled undergrowth. It rewards careful navigation and offers some of the highest biodiversity in the world, hiding rare plant species, lost ruins, and territorial creatures beneath its canopy. A player moves through the jungle slowly and attentively or not at all, reading a place that conceals its dangers and its treasures with equal thoroughness.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

The jungle is built in layers, and exploring it means moving between them: canopy paths overhead, understory routes through the green dark, river corridors that cut the tangle, and root networks that bridge and block in turn. Ancient stonework lies half-swallowed by roots and rainfall throughout, hinting at a history the forest has nearly reclaimed, and the sheer density of growth makes the jungle as easy to get lost in as it is rewarding to chart.

## Resources

The biome's biodiversity is its bounty. Rich resource nodes yield medicinal plants, hardwood, rare pigments, and the spell reagents tied to [Magic](../Magic.md), all feeding the trades of [Crafting](../Crafting.md), and the rarity of some jungle materials makes a successful gathering run genuinely valuable. The catch is that the same density that grows these resources also hides the things that guard them.

## Creatures

The jungle teems with life, and much of it warns before it strikes: many animals use sound, colour, or camouflage as signals a careful player can learn to read. The [Birds](../Creatures/Birds.md) of the canopy are the clearest of these, serving as watchers and alarm signals whose sudden flight betrays a predator or a disturbance below long before it comes into view. Reading the jungle's chorus is as much a survival skill here as any weapon.

## Hazards and Travel

The jungle threatens through ambush and attrition. Ambush predators, poisonous flora, and disorienting terrain make every excursion a test of attention, the high humidity wears at equipment durability over time, and flooded paths and fast river crossings turn deadly during the storms of [Weather, Time, and Seasons](../Weather-time-and-seasons.md). Travel here is slow by necessity, and the player who rushes a river or a blind corridor is the one the jungle takes.

## Signature Landmark

At the jungle's heart stands a major overgrown temple or stepped ruin, a structure that serves as both navigation landmark and dungeon entrance. Its outer terraces support solo exploration and reward the curious, while its deeper sealed chambers open into the kind of group content the [Dungeons](../Structures/Dungeons.md) describe, built around traps, encroaching plant growth, and old magic. The ruin gives the biome a centre and a goal, a place every traveller in the jungle eventually orients toward.
