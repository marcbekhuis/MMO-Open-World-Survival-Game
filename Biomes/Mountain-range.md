# Mountain Range

The Mountain Range is a high-altitude biome of snow-capped peaks, steep passes, and deep ravines, where high winds, sudden storms, and thin air make travel hazardous without the right equipment. It is the harshest of the temperate biomes and the homeland of the dwarves, whose holds and forges are carved into its stone. To cross the mountains is to climb into country that punishes the unprepared and rewards those who come ready for cold, height, and the long exposed routes between one shelter and the next.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

The range is defined by its verticality and its bottlenecks. Peaks and ravines force travel into a handful of passes, which makes roads, bridges, and chokepoints strategically vital and turns control of a pass into control of a region. Scattered through the heights are isolated highland settlements, ancient shrines, and watch-fortresses that cling to defensible ground, and the long sightlines between them mean a traveller is often visible for a great distance, exposed to weather and to anything watching from above.

## Resources

The mountains are rich underground. Mineral-rich veins and caves hold rare ores and crystals, the raw wealth that feeds the forges of [Crafting](../Crafting.md) and that draws the dwarves to drive their tunnels ever deeper. The same mana crystals that the stone giants consume to survive are part of that bounty, which makes the range a place where miners and monsters compete for the same seams.

## Creatures

The signature inhabitant is the [Stone Giant](../Creatures/Stone-giant.md), a colossus of living stone that roams the heights in search of mana crystals and wars with its own kind over them, leaving trails of devastation behind. Hardier ordinary animals such as mountain goats pick their way along the ledges where little else can follow, but it is the giants that define the danger of the high country and that a traveller must learn to route around.

## Hazards and Travel

The mountains threaten in more ways than combat. Avalanches, chasms, and falling rock endanger any route, and the cold exposure and thin air of altitude press hardest on low-endurance characters, draining the reserves of [Survival](../Survival.md) and demanding the right clothing and preparation rather than raw toughness. The same long sightlines that aid navigation also leave travellers open to flying threats, ranged attackers, and the fast weather changes that sweep the peaks.

## Settlement Patterns

The range is dwarven country above all. The stone-hewn holds and forge-centres of [Dwarven Settlements](../Structures/Dwarven-settlements.md), home to the [dwarves](../Races/Dwarf.md), are built for permanence and defence against everything the mountains throw at them, and the highland watch-fortresses and shrines of other cultures hold what defensible ground remains. Settlement here means engineering against the cold, the snow loads, and the exposed passes, which is exactly the discipline the dwarves have built their culture around.
