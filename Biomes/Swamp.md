# Swamp

The Swamp is a dense, humid biome of blackwater channels, reedy marshes, and half-sunken groves, where visibility is poor beneath the heavy canopy and the air carries the scent of peat and rot. It should feel slow, watchful, and dangerous, a place where the ground itself cannot always be trusted and where the real threats are as likely to be the water, the fog, and the fevers as anything with teeth. It is the world's richest source of alchemical materials and its most reliable source of illness, and the two are not unrelated.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

Waterlogged terrain slows travel and rewards amphibious movement, breaking the biome into channels and islands that a traveller must learn rather than simply cross. Half-submerged ruins lie scattered through the marsh, hiding puzzle chambers, flooded cellars, and forgotten shrines, and the narrow boat routes that thread the blackwater reward players who invest in local knowledge or the [Cartography](../Skills/Artisan/Cartography.md) skill to chart them. Beneath the heavy canopy the swamp is dim and close, a place where it is easy to lose the way and easy to be lost track of.

## Resources

For all its menace the swamp is generous to a crafter. It is the prime source of alchemical materials, medicinal herbs, fungal nodes, rare resins, and water-filtering reeds, the reagents that feed the alchemy and medicine of [Crafting](../Crafting.md) and that command good prices in the markets of [Economy and Trade](../Economy.md) precisely because few are willing to wade in after them. The swamp's wealth is a reward for tolerating its dangers, which is the bargain every reagent-gatherer strikes with it.

## Creatures

The marsh hides its predators well. Alligators, swamp spiders, and large water snakes all use the water, reeds, and mud banks to break line of sight before they strike, so the danger here is rarely the one a player can see. The slow-moving country also lies on the migration routes of the [Titan Turtle](../Creatures/Titan-turtle.md), whose immense bulk wades through the shallows and across the soft ground that bears nothing else its size.

## Hazards and Travel

The swamp is the signature home of affliction. Toxic plants and fog apply lingering debuffs, and the damp, the rot, and the standing water breed the fevers and illness that [Survival](../Survival.md) treats as a regional hazard, making the swamp the one biome where disease is a planned-for danger rather than a rare misfortune. The footing is treacherous too, with quicksand, sinkholes, and concealed drop-offs waiting in poorly charted ground, so a traveller here moves slowly, watches the water, and treats the whole biome as a place that wants them to make a mistake.

## Settlement Patterns

Permanent settlement in the swamp is sparse and purpose-built, raised on stilts and lashed platforms above the flood line by those who work the reagents and know the boat routes. It is country settled by specialists rather than by towns, people who accept the fevers and the predators for the wealth the marsh gives up to no one else.
