# Tundra

The Tundra is a frozen forest at the cold edge of the world, where snow lies deep beneath dark evergreens and the cold itself is the first thing that tries to kill a traveller. It is a quiet, beautiful, and unforgiving country, best known as the home of the elusive [Ancient Deer](../Creatures/Ancient-deer.md), whose herds drift through the white silence more felt than seen. Players come here for furs, for mana-rich materials, and for the rare sight of a living legend, and they come prepared for the cold or they do not come back.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

Snow-laden conifers stand over a muffled, trackless floor, and fresh fall erases yesterday's path as surely as the desert's wind erases its dunes. Visibility is the defining variable: on a still day the tundra is luminous and far-seeing, but a blizzard closes the world to an arm's length and turns familiar ground hostile. On the clearest nights the sky carries a faint glow, an echo of the same mana that runs through the deer, and the silence is deep enough that sound itself becomes a way to navigate and to be found.

## Resources

The cold breeds its own wealth. Thick-furred game yields pelts and hides suited to cold-weather gear, hardy herbs push up at the margins of the snow, and clean ice has its own uses, but the prize is the mana-charged body of the Ancient Deer, whose cracked antlers and shimmering coat feed the enchanting and crafting trades. Because the signature creature is so hard to take, tundra materials trade at a premium that rewards the hunters willing to brave both the cold and the herd's storms.

## Creatures

The tundra is the herd-ground of the [Ancient Deer](../Creatures/Ancient-deer.md), peaceful unless threatened and armed with wind and ice when it is, and it is the one creature every visitor hopes and fears to find. Cold-hardy predators range the treeline alongside the deer, drawn by the same game the herds graze among, so a hunter stalking a legend is rarely the only thing hunting in the snow.

## Hazards and Travel

Cold and exposure are the relentless pressures here, draining the survival reserves of [Survival](../Survival.md) and demanding fire, shelter, and the right clothing rather than raw toughness. Blizzards arrive with little warning, as covered in [Weather, Time, and Seasons](../Weather-time-and-seasons.md), erasing visibility and footing and hiding thin ice and sudden drops beneath an even white surface. The deer add a hazard of their own, since a herd that feels cornered can call up a localised storm that turns an incautious hunt into a fight for survival against the weather itself.

## Settlement Patterns

The tundra supports little permanent settlement. What endures is small, hardy, and purposeful: hunters' camps and frontier outposts that exist to work the furs and the rare materials and to shelter those passing through, built low and warm against the wind. Few choose to live here, and those who do are usually after something the cold country guards.
