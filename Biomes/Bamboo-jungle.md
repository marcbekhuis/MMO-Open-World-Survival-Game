# Bamboo Jungle

The Bamboo Jungle is a dense, vertical biome dominated by towering bamboo groves, whose tall, cylindrical trunks create narrow sightlines and natural vertical pathways. It is a biome built for stealth, ambush, and fast repositioning, where the screen of stalks hides as much as it reveals and a traveller is rarely sure what waits a few paces ahead. More than any other biome it rewards awareness over force, because the thing most likely to kill a player here is the one they never saw.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

Movement through the bamboo follows narrow corridors formed by the stalks, with canopy walkways and root bridges offering routes above the ground for those who can reach them. The defining feature is impermanence of sight: when the wind moves the dense bamboo screens, visibility shifts suddenly, opening and closing lines of sight without warning and turning a clear path into a blind one between one gust and the next. The grove is beautiful and disorienting in equal measure, and it never quite lets a traveller settle.

## Resources

The bamboo itself is the biome's wealth. Specialised materials, bamboo fibre, reed sap, and lightweight timber, feed the crafting trades of [Crafting](../Crafting.md), prized for the light, strong gear that suits the stealth and mobility the biome encourages. Harvesting them means working among the stalks where the biome's predators wait, so even gathering here is an exercise in vigilance.

## Creatures

The signature resident is the [Bamboo Spider](../Creatures/Bamboo-spider.md), a large ambush predator whose long, slender legs mimic the bamboo so closely that it all but vanishes among the stalks. It uses the vertical surfaces and dense screens to close distance unseen, which makes it the embodiment of the biome's central danger and the reason a careful player reads the grove for the wrongness that betrays a hunter lying in wait.

## Hazards and Travel

The bamboo jungle is a biome of ambush. Concealed traps and the spiders' hunting grounds make the deep groves perilous, and the wind-driven shifts in visibility mean a traveller can lose sight of a threat, or of the way out, at any moment. The answer is not strength but caution: moving deliberately, watching for the telltale stillness where the grove should be alive, and refusing to be hurried through country that punishes haste.

## Settlement Patterns

What settlement exists here is small, hidden, and built upward, with villages raised on stilts, lashed platforms, and suspended bridges above the flood-prone ground. These communities use the same verticality the predators do, keeping above the danger and out of easy sight, and they belong to people who have made their peace with a biome that never fully reveals itself.
