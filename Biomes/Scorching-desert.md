# Scorching Desert

The Scorching Desert is the harshest open biome in the world, a place where the heat of the sun is a greater enemy than anything that lives there. Rolling dunes run to the horizon with no water in sight, and the few creatures hardy enough to call it home, great insects and the burrowing [Sandworm](../Creatures/Sandworm.md) chief among them, only make the emptiness more dangerous. It is a barren wasteland most travellers go out of their way to avoid, and the ones who cross it anyway do so for what the open dunes hide rather than for the crossing itself.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

The desert is a sea of shifting sand with almost nothing to fix the eye on, which makes navigation a real problem: dunes change between visits, heat-haze swallows the middle distance, and a traveller who loses their bearings can wander in circles until their water runs out. The sun punishes everything by day, and the same cloudless sky that bakes the dunes lets all that heat escape after dark, so nights turn bitterly, dangerously cold. The biome borders the broken plateaus of the [Mesa](Mesa.md), and the transition from canyon shade to open dune is where most expeditions stage, resupply, and turn back.

## Resources

What the desert offers is scarce and therefore valuable. The body of a slain sandworm yields rare materials prized for high-tier and heat-resistant gear, sun-hardy desert flora supports specialist alchemy, and the wind that erases landmarks also strips sand from buried ruins and exposes mineral veins that lie hidden elsewhere. Water itself is the most precious resource of all, so that control of a spring or an oasis is worth more here than a vein of ore would be in gentler country.

## Creatures

The dunes belong to the [Sandworm](../Creatures/Sandworm.md), the blind, vibration-hunting apex that makes the open desert lethal, and to the large insects and scavengers that have adapted to the heat and the scarcity. Life is thin on the ground precisely because so little can survive the conditions, which means almost anything a traveller does meet is either a specialist worth studying or a predator worth fearing.

## Hazards and Travel

Heat and dehydration are the constant threats, draining the survival reserves covered in [Survival](../Survival.md) faster than any other biome, and the swing to night cold punishes anyone who packed only for the day. Sudden sandstorms strip visibility and footing, as detailed in [Weather, Time, and Seasons](../Weather-time-and-seasons.md), and there is almost no natural shelter to wait one out. Above all there is the worm, drawn by the steady rhythm of marching feet or galloping hooves, so the safest crossings are made on bare rock where it cannot follow, in broken and irregular movement, and in the cooler hours when the sand is still.

## Settlement Patterns

Permanent settlement inside the deep desert is rare to the point of legend; the country simply will not support it. What takes hold instead clings to the margins, the prospector camps and trader outposts at the mesa border, and the fiercely defended oasis settlements that grow up around the few reliable sources of water. To hold water in the desert is to hold power over everyone who must cross it.
