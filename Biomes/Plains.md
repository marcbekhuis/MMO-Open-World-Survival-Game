# Plains

The Plains are one of the world's gentlest, most beginner-friendly biomes, vast open grasslands that stretch as far as the eye can see. Rolling fields dotted with occasional flowerbeds give the country its calm, colourful character, while rivers running down from distant mountains weave through the grass and line their banks with denser foliage, creating lush green oases amid the open expanse. The tranquil beauty, abundant resources, and relative safety of the plains make them an ideal place for a new adventurer to find their feet.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

Wide horizons and gentle ground define the plains, and the openness is itself the biome's signature: there is little to hide a traveller and little to hide from them, so threats are seen coming and routes are easy to read. The riverside oases break the grassland into landmarks, and their slightly denser foliage offers the only real cover and the richest pockets of life in an otherwise open country.

## Resources

The river oases teem with life and make the plains generous to a gatherer. Herds of grazing animals support hunting, the watered banks favour foraging and early herbalism, and the rivers themselves carry fish and fresh water down from the mountains. Nothing here is rare or contested the way desert water or tundra furs are, which is exactly what makes the plains a forgiving place to learn the gathering and survival loop before carrying it into harsher country.

## Creatures

The plains are home to the harmless and the familiar: rabbits, horses, hardy highland cattle, and the [Birds](../Creatures/Birds.md) that fill the open sky, alongside the occasional wandering [Slime](../Creatures/Slime.md). The one genuinely awe-inspiring resident is the [Titan Turtle](../Creatures/Titan-turtle.md), whose slow, unstoppable migrations cross the flat country and which the people of the plains learn simply to live around rather than to fight.

## Hazards and Travel

The plains are safe by the standards of this world, not by the standards of a tutorial. Travel is easy and visibility is good, but the same openness that reveals danger also leaves a traveller exposed, with little cover when a predator or a rival player does appear. The greatest hazard is the largest: a titan turtle on the move flattens whatever lies in its path, so the smart traveller treats the giants as terrain to be routed around rather than obstacles to be challenged.

## Settlement Patterns

The plains suit settlement well, and human towns in particular take root here, along the rivers where water, grazing, and easy travel meet. The biome's safety and openness make it a natural place for the roadside villages and growing towns described in [Human Settlements](../Structures/Human-settlements.md), and for the early bases of players still learning what the frontier costs.
