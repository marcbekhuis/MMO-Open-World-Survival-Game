# Mesa

The Mesa forms a rugged borderland around the desert regions, defined by towering plateaus, steep cliffs, dry basins, and deep canyons. It is not as scorching or as barren as the deepest [Scorching Desert](Scorching-desert.md), but limited water and sparse vegetation still make travel demanding, and the broken terrain turns every journey into a question of which route through the rock is passable. It should read as an opportunity-rich frontier: harder than the plains, kinder than the dunes, and worth the risk for what its stone holds.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

Tall rock formations dominate the mesa, serving as natural landmarks that orient a traveller and as ambush points that endanger them in equal measure. Movement follows the shape of the land, along canyon roads, rope bridges, cliffside paths, and the exposed mining trails that cling to the rock, so a crossing here is rarely direct and always exposed. The result is a biome where knowing the routes is as valuable as being able to fight along them.

## Resources

The mesa's wealth is in its stone. Valuable metal deposits run through it, including copper, iron, and rarer veins hidden deep in the canyon walls, which is what draws prospectors and miners to such unforgiving country in the first place. Extracting that wealth means working dangerous sites in exposed places, so the mesa rewards the prepared and punishes the greedy.

## Creatures

The cliffs and canyons are the territory of dragons, with the [Royal Dragon](../Creatures/Royal-dragon.md) the apex among the species that nest in the high rock, alongside lesser cliff predators that hunt the ledges and trails. A traveller in the mesa shares the sky with things that regard the canyons as their own, which adds a vertical dimension to danger that the open plains never have.

## Hazards and Travel

Heat, dehydration, sudden dust storms, and long stretches without shelter make the mesa a genuine survival test, milder than the desert but unforgiving to the unprepared. The terrain adds its own threats: cliff predators strike from above, raiders who know the canyon routes prey on those who do not, and the mining sites that make the biome valuable are also its most lethal ground, where cave-ins, unstable ledges, and monster lairs overlap. Routes, water, and timing all have to be planned rather than improvised.

## Settlement Patterns

The mesa is settled by those with nowhere gentler to go: outcasts, prospectors, traders, and wanderers from many cultures, who raise small communities in defensible pockets of the rock. These mixed settlements are a rich source of quests and friction, mining disputes, caravan protection, hidden outlaw camps, and the fragile alliances struck between people the rest of the world has pushed to its margins.
