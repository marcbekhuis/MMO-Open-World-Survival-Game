# Forest

The Forest is a temperate, mixed-woodland biome that sits between the open plains and the deeper wildlands, and for most players it is where the real game begins. It is generous enough to be forgiving and dangerous enough to teach, offering abundant resources, natural shelter, and lore-rich ruins to early and mid-game players while quietly schooling them in the habits the harsher country will demand. A player who learns to read the forest is being prepared, whether they know it or not, for everything beyond it.

## Terrain and Atmosphere

The woodland is laced with natural corridors, clearings, and ridgelines that make it a readable place to travel, and that readability is the point: the forest is where players learn how sound, tracks, and landmarks support navigation, lessons that carry into biomes far less willing to forgive a wrong turn. Hidden groves, old cairns, and minor puzzle ruins break up the canopy and reward the curious, giving the forest a sense of quiet history beneath its green.

## Resources

The forest is rich and accessible to a gatherer, with harvestable timber, plentiful wild game, and foraging nodes scattered through the undergrowth. Nothing here is as rare or as fiercely guarded as the prizes of the harsher biomes, which is exactly what makes the forest the right place to practise the gathering and crafting loop before carrying it into more dangerous country.

## Creatures

The forest's signature threat is the [Wolf](../Creatures/Wolf.md), whose packs teach a player that the wilderness fights as a group rather than as a single foe. Bands of [Goblins](../Creatures/Goblin.md) raid its edges, the occasional [Slime](../Creatures/Slime.md) drifts through its hollows, and the [Birds](../Creatures/Birds.md) overhead give constant, readable warning of what moves beneath the trees. Rarely, the slow migration of a [Titan Turtle](../Creatures/Titan-turtle.md) turns a stretch of woodland into a path of splintered trunks and deep footprints, while rabbits and deer make up the ordinary game a hunter relies on.

## Hazards and Travel

For all its generosity the forest has teeth. Territorial predators and occasional bandit activity make the deeper woods a place to stay alert, and dense undergrowth hides traps and ambush points that punish the careless. The seasons reach into the forest as much as anywhere, and the shifting weather of [Weather, Time, and Seasons](../Weather-time-and-seasons.md) can change visibility, footing, and what the woodland offers from one visit to the next.

## Settlement Patterns

The forest supports a scattering of modest habitation: travelling merchant routes thread through it, and small cabin settlements take hold in its clearings, offering a player the occasional outpost of safety and trade without ever feeling as secure as a town on the open plains. It is country settled lightly, by people who want the forest's resources and accept its risks.
