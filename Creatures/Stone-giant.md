# Stone Giant

Stone Giants are colossal beings of living stone, animated by the mana they draw from the crystals buried in the mountains. Mana is their lifeblood: fully charged, a giant regenerates over time, sealing the cracks and fractures in its stony flesh; starved of it, it slowly crumbles, and if its reserves run out entirely it collapses into a heap of mineral-rich rubble that daring scavengers prize. They are among the elder beasts that [Setting and Lore](../Setting-and-lore.md) counts as pieces of the world's own myth, and they roam the high country of the [Mountain Range](../Biomes/Mountain-range.md) in a perpetual search for the crystals that keep them alive.

## Appearance and Visual Design

A stone giant looks as though a mountainside has remembered how to stand. Its body is built from overlapping strata of granite, basalt, shale, and quartz, with broad shoulders like broken cliffs and limbs that do not bend so much as grind from one locked angle to another. Smaller giants retain a recognisable humanoid outline, while the oldest become irregular and monumental, with ridges for brows, terraces for backs, and arms uneven enough to suggest pieces of different peaks fused together over a very long life.

Mana crystals are the only strong colour in the design. They grow in seams along the spine, shoulders, knuckles, and chest, glowing blue-white or violet from within the rock when the giant is healthy. A starving giant loses that inner light first: crystals dull, cracks widen, gravel sloughs from its joints, and dry dust pours from wounds the way blood would from flesh. Moss, alpine grass, lichen pockets, and wind-bent shrubs cling to elder specimens, so a sleeping giant can be mistaken for a ridge until the crystals under the stone pulse and the ridge turns its head.

## Living Stone and Mana

A giant's size is a measure of its age and its appetite. They range from five to a hundred metres tall, and the larger and older a giant grows, the more mana crystals it needs to sustain itself, which sets them against one another: fights for mana erupt between giants, and the victor absorbs what remains of the loser to extend its own life. The oldest become walking landscapes, their backs grown over with moss, grass, bushes, and in truly ancient specimens whole trees, so that an elder stone giant reads less as a monster than as a moving piece of the mountain itself.

## Fighting a Stone Giant

A stone giant cannot be fought like a living thing, and a sword accomplishes little against it. The reliable approach is a miner's: chip away at the shell with a sturdy pickaxe to expose the crystalline heart hidden within, then strike that. Reaching it is the challenge, since the heart sits high on a body that resists every attempt to scale it, crushing makeshift platforms and swatting away climbers. Some players take the patient route instead and starve a giant of mana, denying it the crystals it needs and letting it crumble; others mount a direct assault, climbing with grappling hooks and built platforms to reach the heart while the giant does everything in its power to throw them off. Cannons and catapults can wear a giant down, but the killing blow almost always comes from the pickaxe at the heart.

## Story Hook

A mining settlement in the high passes has prospered for years by quietly feeding crystals to an old stone giant that wanders nearby, keeping it docile and far from their tunnels. Now a larger giant has entered the region, the two have begun to fight over the crystal seams the settlement depends on, and the miners face a choice: pick a side in a war between mountains, find a way to drive both giants off, or abandon a hold that may not survive the next clash.
