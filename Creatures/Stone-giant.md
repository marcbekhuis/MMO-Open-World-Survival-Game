Stone Giants are colossal beings composed of living stone, powered by the magical energy they draw from mana crystals. Without a steady stream of mana, they slowly crumble, losing their ability to mend the rock that forms their bodies. Conversely, when fully charged, they regenerate over time, sealing cracks and fractures in their stony flesh. If their mana reserves are completely depleted, they collapse into a heap of mineral-rich rubble, sought after by daring scavengers.
Rather than relying on swords, adventurers find that a sturdy pickaxe proves most effective against these titans, allowing them to chip away at the Stone Giant’s shell and expose the crystalline heart hidden within. Some choose to starve them of mana, preventing them from replenishing their energy, while others mount a direct assault by scaling the giant’s body to strike at its heart. These towering creatures can vary in height from five to one hundred meters, with the larger, older giants requiring ever-increasing amounts of mana crystals to survive. Fights for mana often erupt between them, and when one giant is slain, the victor absorbs the remaining mana, extending its own life.
While cannons and catapults can damage these towering behemoths, the precision offered by a pickaxe remains the most reliable method of delivering a killing blow. Adventurers can climb, use grappling hooks, or even build makeshift platforms on the giant’s body to ascend and reach its vulnerable heart. However, the creature fiercely resists these tactics, trying to crush the structures on its surface and swat away anyone attempting to scale it.
Stone Giants roam in search of mana crystals, battling one another for survival and leaving trails of devastation behind them as they trample anything in their path. Over time, older giants become covered in moss, grass, and bushes, and in the case of truly ancient specimens, even entire trees can grow upon their backs, adding to their imposing, otherworldly presence.
