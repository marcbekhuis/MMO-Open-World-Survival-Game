# Skeleton Knight

Skeleton Knights are the armoured front line of the undead that hold the world's [Dungeons](../Structures/Dungeons.md). They are not wandering monsters but fixtures of the places that made them: the dungeon itself animates and sustains them, and the binding does not reach past the threshold. A skeleton knight that takes a single step into open air collapses into a heap of bones, which is why these creatures are never met in the wilderness and never have to be fought anywhere but on their own ground.

## Behaviour

A skeleton knight fights as a disciplined melee bulwark, the anchor that a dungeon's defence is built around. It holds chokepoints, absorbs punishment behind shield and plate, and presses forward with a patience that the living rarely match, untroubled by fear, fatigue, or pain. On its own it is a manageable threat for a prepared player; in the tight corridors and guarded vaults of a dungeon, paired with ranged support, it becomes the wall that pins a party in place while other things close in. Because it cannot be lured outside to be fought on easier terms, the only way past is through, on terrain the dungeon has shaped to the knight's advantage.

## Story Hook

A sealed lower vault is guarded by a knight that does not attack intruders so much as refuse them, stepping back into the doorway each time it is pushed aside, never pursuing, never tiring. Adventurers who have faced it come away certain it is guarding something rather than merely killing for the dungeon, and the question of what a bound, mindless thing could still be choosing to protect is enough to draw a steady trickle of the curious down into the dark.
