# Crafting

Crafting is how raw material taken from the world becomes the gear, food, structures, and goods that play runs on. For a player who wants it, crafting is a deep and central pursuit, the spine of the self-sufficient frontier life and a direct route into the player economy. For a player who does not, it is entirely skippable, because the NPC craftsmen of every settlement keep a non-crafter fully equipped. This document covers how things are made, how good they can be, and how player production sits alongside the goods NPCs already turn out. What those goods are then worth is a question for [Economy and Trade](Economy.md); this page is about making them.

## Gathering and Refinement

Crafting begins in the wild. The gathering disciplines of [Skills](Skills.md), logging, mining, harvesting, skinning, fishing, and salvage, pull raw material out of the world, and each biome offers its own: ore from the [Mountain Range](Biomes/Mountain-range.md), reagents from the [Swamp](Biomes/Swamp.md), timber and game from the forests, and rarer materials from the bodies of the world's great creatures. Most raw material is refined before it is useful, ore smelted to metal, hide tanned to leather, herbs reduced to reagents, so that production runs in stages from the gatherer through the refiner to the maker. Because everything carried has weight under the rules in [Inventory](Inventory.md), hauling materials home is itself a logistical decision, and the gap between where a material is found and where it is worked is part of what gives it value.

## Stations and Workshops

Serious crafting happens at stations, and stations live in the structures players raise through the [Building System](Building-system.md). A forge, a tannery, an alchemy bench, or a cooking fire each enables its own kind of work, and the better the station the better the work it supports, so a well-appointed workshop is a real investment that turns a base into a place of production rather than mere shelter. This ties crafting directly to settlement: the forge districts of a dwarven hold described in [Dwarven Settlements](Structures/Dwarven-settlements.md) are crafting infrastructure at civic scale, and the reason other players travel to dwarven territory to buy what is made there. A player without stations of their own can still use those a settlement provides, which keeps crafting open to the city-dweller as well as the frontier holdout.

## Quality and Specialization

Player-made goods are not uniform. Quality scales with the maker's skill, the grade of the materials, and the station used, so the same recipe in two different hands produces two different results, and a master's work commands a price an apprentice's cannot. This is what lets players carve out a place the NPC economy leaves open: vendors and NPC craftsmen turn out reliable, ordinary gear, but the finest equipment, the largest backpacks, the rare magic pouches of [Inventory](Inventory.md), and goods made in quantities NPCs never reach all come from specialised players. Recipes and patterns themselves are something to be earned, bought from trainers, discovered through the artisan skills, or recovered as treasure from the depths of a [dungeon](Structures/Dungeons.md), so a crafter's repertoire is a record of where they have been as much as what they have practised.

## Magic Items and Enchanting

The highest tier of crafting binds the world's own force into objects. In a setting where magic wells from the world itself, the making of enchanted weapons, armour, and tools is its own discipline, drawing on rare leyline-rich materials and the traditions of [Magic](Magic.md), and the mana-charged bodies of creatures like the [Ancient Deer](Creatures/Ancient-deer.md) are sought precisely as components for it. No people are better at this than the dwarves: as set out in [Dwarf](Races/Dwarf.md), they answer their own poor affinity for casting by forging power into steel, making them the world's foremost producers of magic items and giving even a non-caster a way to carry real magic into a fight.

## Crafting Is Optional, by Design

Crafting is built so that a player can make everything they use, or none of it. The NPC craftsmen and vendors of [Economy and Trade](Economy.md) keep a player who never crafts an item fully supplied, which makes the city merchant and the trade-only adventurer of [Player Progression](Player.md) complete ways to play rather than handicapped ones. What crafting adds for those who pursue it is reach and independence: the ability to outfit oneself in the field far from any vendor, to make better goods than money can readily buy, and to feed the constant demand created by the full-loot deaths of [Death and Loss](Death-and-loss.md), which destroy and recirculate gear faster than NPCs alone could ever replace it. Crafting is central without being mandatory, exactly as the design intends.
