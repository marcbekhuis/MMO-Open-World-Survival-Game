# Travel and Mounts

Travel is not the space between the game's content; it is the content. The seamless world of [World Design](World.md) is built so that crossing it matters, with distance, terrain, and danger turning a journey into a thing a player prepares for and remembers. Faster means of travel exist and are worth earning, but the design holds to a single rule: every one of them should expand the value of travel rather than erase it. This document gathers the ways a player moves through the world, from their own two feet to the back of a flying beast, and how each fits that rule.

## On Foot and the Value of Distance

The baseline is travel on foot, and most of the world is crossed that way, at least at first. Walking keeps the player inside the survival pressures of [Survival](Survival.md), the food carried, the fatigue accrued, the weather endured, and inside the open danger of [Conflict and PvP](Conflict-and-pvp.md), where every stretch of road is a risk taken in the open. This is deliberate: because distance has weight, the landmarks, routes, and shortcuts a player learns become real knowledge, and the cartography skill of [Cartography](Skills/Artisan/Cartography.md) turns that knowledge into something other players will pay for. Faster travel is measured against this baseline, and earns its place only by being limited, costly, or hard to reach.

## Mounts

A mount is the first real answer to distance, and here it is a living creature rather than a vehicle conjured from a menu. Mounts are tamed through the methods set out in [Taming and Control](Taming-and-control.md), and the strength of that bond decides how well the animal answers under saddle, so a mount is something a player wins and maintains rather than simply owns. A good mount carries its rider faster and can bear extra load against the weight limits of [Inventory](Inventory.md), making mounted players the backbone of caravans and the trade routes of [Economy and Trade](Economy.md). A mount is also a liability: it is vulnerable, it can be killed or taken, and its steady gait across open ground is exactly the rhythm that draws a [Sandworm](Creatures/Sandworm.md) up through the dunes. Different country suits different mounts, and no single animal is at home everywhere.

## Sailing and the Sea

Water is its own domain of travel. Ships open the oceans and coasts to exploration, trade, and war, carrying cargo in bulk no land caravan can match and reaching places the land routes never touch. The sea exacts its own price: the storms of [Weather, Time, and Seasons](Weather-time-and-seasons.md) make open water treacherous, and the same severe weather that endangers a ship is what draws the [Kraken](Creatures/Kraken.md) up from the deep, so a sea crossing is a gamble against both the sky and what lives beneath it. A laden merchant ship is as tempting a target on the water as a caravan is on the road, which ties sailing into the same web of risk and reward as travel everywhere else.

## Climbing and Verticality

The world is built upward as much as outward, and reaching its heights is a travel problem in itself. Players descend into caves and ruins, scale the ridges and exposed faces of the [Mountain Range](Biomes/Mountain-range.md), and pick their way up cliffs to reach ground that cannot be walked to. Climbing draws on stamina and on the survival skills that keep a climber from a fatal fall, and it rewards the effort with vantage, shortcuts, and access to places most travellers never see. Verticality is what keeps the world from being a flat map: the most valuable ground is often the hardest to reach, and reaching it is part of the achievement.

## Flight

Flight is the rarest and most coveted form of travel, the only way to reach the highest country and the late-game prize that opens the world fully. The [Floating Islands](Biomes/Floating-islands.md) can be reached no other way, and a player who masters a flying mount, one of the great winged creatures of the bestiary such as the [Flying Leviathan](Creatures/Flying-leviathan.md) or a [Royal Dragon](Creatures/Royal-dragon.md), commands a freedom of movement nothing on the ground can match. That power is gated hard, behind taming creatures that are themselves apex threats, so flight is something a player works toward across a long stretch of the game rather than a convenience handed out early. The synchronization demands of fast, high-flying entities are part of why the design treats them carefully in [Dynamic Culling and Render Distance](Dynamic-culling-and-render-distance.md).

## Portals and Express Routes

The fastest travel of all is magical, and the most strictly limited. The portals that link the capital cities, described among the [Structures](Structures.md), can move a player across the world in an instant, but they are priced and constrained so that they serve to reunite friends across distant starts rather than to replace the ordinary movement of people and goods. As one of the deliberate currency sinks of [Economy and Trade](Economy.md), a portal charges a toll meaningful enough that trade still travels the long way, and cooldowns or restrictions on what can be carried keep them from collapsing the world's distances. Express travel exists to make the vast world navigable without making it small.
