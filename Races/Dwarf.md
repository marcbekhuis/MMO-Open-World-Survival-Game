# Dwarf

Dwarves are the forge-bred people of the mountains and the deep places, built around strength, resilience, and an obsessive mastery of craft. A dwarf begins hardier than the other races and more at home in heavy armour and close, grinding combat, the kind of fighter who is hardest to move and hardest to put down. What sets the race apart is not raw power, though, but what dwarves do with their hands: in a world where magic wells from the world itself, they are the ones who learn to bind it into steel.

## Strengths and Playstyle

A dwarf's natural lean is toward strength, endurance, melee durability, and the disciplines of crafting, the attributes and skills laid out in [Player Progression](../Player.md) and the artisan lines of [Skills](../Skills.md). They make natural front-line fighters and siege-breakers, holding ground while lighter races dart and circle. Their weakness is the other side of the same coin: dwarves have almost no innate affinity for casting, and a dwarf who tries to out-magic an elf on raw talent will lose. The race rewards players who like to be the immovable point in a fight and the one who armed everyone else for it.

## Masters of the Forge

What dwarves lack in casting they have more than recovered in craft. They are the world's foremost makers of magic items, able to forge weapons, armour, and tools that carry power a dwarf could never channel unaided. This is the race's defining answer to its own weakness: a dwarf in heavy plate with a strong enchanted weapon can stand against a mage and win, not by matching spell for spell but by closing the distance and never falling. That mastery also gives dwarven holds their pull on the rest of the world, since the forge districts described in [Dwarven Settlements](../Structures/Dwarven-settlements.md) draw other players into dwarven territory to trade for what only dwarves make well.

## Homeland and Start

Dwarves begin in the deep gates and forge halls of a mountain hold, with early tasks that teach endurance, mining, heavy armour, and the value of preparation before venturing into exposed passes or deep caves. Their homeland is the [Mountain Range](../Biomes/Mountain-range.md) and the tunnels beneath it, and their realm pushes its holds and forges into the frontier's stone as set out in [Setting and Lore](../Setting-and-lore.md). A dwarf who wants to play against type can still become a capable caster with enough investment in the right stats and skills; the race simply makes them work harder for it than an elf would, and rewards them with the durability to survive the lessons.
