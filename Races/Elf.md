# Elf

Elves are the agile, precise, and magically gifted people of the ancient forests, drawn to the places where the world's force runs closest to the surface. An elf begins quicker and more perceptive than the other races, suited to ranged combat, stealth, and the careful reading of terrain, and stands nearer to magic than anyone else at the start. What they give up for that closeness is raw physical mass: an elf will not out-muscle a dwarf, and an elf caught in a straight melee against a heavier foe is fighting on the wrong terms.

## Strengths and Playstyle

The elven lean is toward agility, precision, stealth, ranged combat, and magical affinity, the attributes set out in [Player Progression](../Player.md). They excel as scouts, archers, and casters, controlling a fight from distance and angles rather than from the front line, and their perception rewards players who read the ground before committing to it. The trade-off is fragility in the wrong situation; an elf who is forced into a grinding, toe-to-toe fight is playing against their own strengths. At full power the magic more than compensates, and a strong elven mage drawing on the leylines can break an unprepared force on their own, scattering an army of soldiers who came expecting a fair fight.

## Closeness to Magic

Where dwarves bind magic into objects, elves channel it directly. Their affinity is cultural as much as innate, sharpened by living among the leyline-rich and the primeval, and elven settlements are built around that relationship: the nature-magic sanctuaries and quiet libraries of [Elven Settlements](../Structures/Elven-settlements.md) are where much of the world's casting tradition is kept and taught. An elf's path through [Magic](../Magic.md) opens faster and reaches further than another race's, which makes the race the natural choice for a player who wants spellcasting to be the spine of their character rather than a side discipline.

## Homeland and Start

Elves begin in one of the great forest cities, surrounded by towering trees and quiet routes into the deep woodland, with an early experience that teaches vertical navigation, careful movement, ranged combat, and the value of reading environmental cues. Their homeland is the [Ancient Forest](../Biomes/Ancient-forest.md), and their realm is drawn outward toward the charged, primeval corners of the frontier described in [Setting and Lore](../Setting-and-lore.md). An elf who wants to play against type can build toward heavier combat with enough investment, trading some of the race's natural reach for staying power, though they will always have to work for the resilience a dwarf is simply born with.
