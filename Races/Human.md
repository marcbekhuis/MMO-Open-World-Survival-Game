# Human

Humans are the adaptable baseline of the world, strong at nothing by birth and capable of anything with time. A human begins with balanced attributes and no pronounced affinity, which reads at first like a disadvantage beside the dwarf's resilience or the elf's gift for magic, but is in fact the race's defining strength: nothing about a human pulls them toward one role, so every role stays open. They are the settlers and opportunists of the frontier, the people most likely to plant a town where others see only risk.

## Strengths and Playstyle

A human's balanced spread across the attributes in [Player Progression](../Player.md) supports melee, magic, crafting, trade, or any hybrid of them without penalty. Where a dwarf or elf reaches their natural specialisation quickly and cheaply, a human can reach any specialisation given more time and more investment, and can just as easily build the kind of cross-trained character that the other races find awkward. The cost is that a human is rarely the best in the world at the thing they choose; the reward is that they are never shut out of it, and that a human who commits fully can still stand among the finest in any single discipline. The race suits players who want their identity to come entirely from how they play rather than from where they started.

## A Culture of Adaptation

Human strength is social as much as personal. Their realm survives by trade, alliance, engineering, and the flexible use of whatever local materials a place offers, and their settlements show it: the roadside villages, fortified towns, and busy capital cities of [Human Settlements](../Structures/Human-settlements.md) range wider in form than any other race's, shaped to their surroundings rather than to a single architectural ideal. That same adaptability makes humans the natural backbone of the player economy described in [Economy and Trade](../Economy.md), where a city merchant who never raises a wall or forges a blade can still thrive purely on buying and selling.

## Homeland and Start

Humans begin in one of the largest human cities, hubs that connect to capital-level trade and the portal network, with early quests tied to caravans, roads, monster pressure, and the ordinary business of a settlement holding its ground. They are not bound to any one biome; human towns take root from plains to river valleys to mining roads, and their realm pushes into the frontier as the most opportunistic of the three set out in [Setting and Lore](../Setting-and-lore.md). For a human there is no playing against type, because there is no type to play against. Every direction is the intended one.
