# Thunder

Thunder is a lightning spell that calls a storm-charged strike onto a targeted area. It should feel powerful, loud, and risky: a spell that rewards setup and timing more than constant casting.

## Gameplay Role

Thunder is best used against clustered enemies, large creatures, siege targets, or opponents forced into predictable movement. Its impact can deal heavy lightning damage and briefly disrupt actions, but the spell should have a visible warning window so attentive players can dodge, block, or interrupt the caster.

## Upgrade Directions

Thunder can be shaped toward faster strikes, conductive spread, focused damage, or sustained storm pressure. Strike speed reduces the delay between targeting and impact. Conductive spread improves chaining through wet ground, metal armour, or storm conditions. Focused bolt narrows the area for higher single-target damage. Storm call raises cost and cooldown in exchange for repeated strikes over a short duration.

## Trade-Offs

Increasing power should raise mana cost, casting time, or environmental risk. In dry conditions the spell may be less reliable, while rain, high altitude, and open water can make it more threatening.
