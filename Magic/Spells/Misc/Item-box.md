# Item Box

The Item Box is a powerful late-game spell that gives a mage a private store of their own, a pocket of magical space that only the caster can reach. It functions as a personal vault carried in the weave of the spell rather than on the body, and for a player heavily invested in magic it offers a kind of convenience and security no physical container can match. It is one of the defining rewards of a committed caster, and a direct answer to the harshest stakes of the world.

## Gameplay Role

The Item Box's value rests on three properties. Its contents are accessible to the caster alone, so what is stored in it is genuinely private and secure. What it holds sits outside the carry weight of [Inventory](../../../Inventory.md), letting a mage keep essentials on hand without being encumbered by them. Above all, its contents are not lost on death: as set out in [Death and Loss](../../../Death-and-loss.md), the Item Box never appears on the corpse, which makes it the one reliable way to carry something into danger without wagering it, and a large part of why the spell is so prized. Together these make the Item Box indispensable to a late-game mage running dangerous expeditions.

## Upgrade Directions

The spell grows through invested spell points, and its upgrades are build choices rather than flat increases. A caster can expand the number of slots the box holds, trading depth in this spell against everything else their points could buy; reduce the spell's continuous mana drain to make long-term storage sustainable; or unlock preservation, which keeps food and other perishables from spoiling inside the box and turns it into the ideal larder for the long survival expeditions of [Survival](../../../Survival.md). Each direction shapes the box toward a different role, the deep vault, the always-on convenience, or the expedition pantry.

## Trade-Offs

The Item Box is kept in check by mana. Opening it to reach the contents costs a significant amount of mana up front, and while items remain stored the spell drains mana continuously, with the drain scaling by the number and type of items held, so a mage cannot simply pour their wealth into it and forget the cost. Its slots are finite, expandable only through upgrades, and the whole spell is gated behind the mana economy of [Magic](../../../Magic.md). These limits are deliberate: they keep physical storage and the carrying decisions of [Inventory](../../../Inventory.md) relevant rather than letting one spell make them obsolete.
