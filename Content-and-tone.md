# Content and Tone

This document sets the maturity ceiling and the tonal contract for the game: what the world is willing to show, how brutal combat reads, and where the lines are drawn. It exists so that every other system, from the bestiary to [Combat Skills](Skills/Combat.md) to the quests that play out in settlements, pulls in the same direction. The intended experience is a grim, adult survival world in which violence carries weight and consequence rather than serving as spectacle for its own sake, and the title is built throughout to a Mature standard.

## Target Rating and Audience

The game targets an adult audience and is designed to an ESRB Mature 17+ and PEGI 18 standard. Its player is someone who wants a harsh, grounded fantasy where the wilderness can kill them and a lost fight costs something real. The deliberate line that keeps the game at Mature rather than Adults Only is sexual content: none is depicted, as set out below. Holding that one line preserves the widest viable distribution, console certification and mainstream storefronts included, without softening any of the violence or themes that give the world its character. An age gate at account creation and clear content descriptors at install are assumed parts of the package.

## Violence and Gore

Combat is visceral and physical. Blows land with weight, wounds bleed and spatter, blood pools where bodies fall, and limbs can be severed and heads taken. The dead leave corpses on the ground until they decay or are cleared, so a hard-fought field still looks like one minutes later. Killing strikes can resolve as brutal finishers suited to the weapon in hand, and a cleaving two-hander parts an enemy very differently than a dagger does. None of this is reserved for humanoids: a [Royal Dragon](Creatures/Royal-dragon.md) brought down by a coordinated raid and a lone [Goblin](Creatures/Goblin.md) cut apart on a forest path both read as real, costly violence. Seen down the length of the player's own arms in first person, that violence is immediate in a way a detached camera would blunt.

Gore here serves the design rather than shock. It reinforces the weight and consequence at the centre of survival combat, because a fight that can take your arm is a fight you prepare for, scout, and sometimes choose to avoid. [Combat Skills](Skills/Combat.md) carry this through in their finishers and impact upgrades, and individual bestiary entries describe wounding appropriate to each species. Players who prefer a less intense presentation can scale the effect down through [Player Settings](Player-settings.md) without altering the rules underneath.

## Nudity and Sexuality

The game permits optional nudity. It is off by default and controlled entirely by the local player through [Player Settings](Player-settings.md), which makes it a presentation choice on the viewer's own client rather than a social act imposed on anyone else. Mature relationships, attraction, and suggestive writing have a place in the world and in how its characters speak, but no sexual act is depicted. This is the boundary that keeps the title at Mature and PEGI 18 instead of Adults Only, and the design holds it on purpose.

## A Grim World, Not a Cruelty Sandbox

The world is dark and does not look away from cruelty. Slavery, torture, massacre, cannibal cults, ritual sacrifice, and the long aftermath of war live in its history, its factions, and its environmental storytelling, and a roadside cage, a sacked village, or an NPC slaver caravan are all fair material for quests and for the [Reputation System](Reputation-system.md). What the world is willing to depict, though, is not the same as what players may do to one another. Conflict between players is lethal combat, not a cruelty toolkit: players cannot capture, enslave, torture, or hold one another prisoner. The grimmest acts belong to the world and its NPC antagonists, where they carry authored meaning, rather than being handed to players as systems to turn on each other. What a defeated player actually loses is a question for the rules in [Death and Loss](Death-and-loss.md), not for any capture mechanic.

## Substances and Vice

Drink and narcotics are part of the texture of an adult world without being a mechanical subsystem. Taverns serve real ale, frontier apothecaries and back-alley dealers trade in worse, and dialogue and lore treat intoxication and addiction honestly rather than coyly. These things stay flavour: they colour places, characters, and stories, but they grant no buffs or debuffs and drive no addiction loop, so the survival meters a player has to manage are food, fatigue, and the hazards of the world rather than a craving bar.

## Player Comfort and Content Controls

Because the default presentation is deliberately intense, players decide how much of it reaches their own screen. Gore intensity and the optional nudity toggle live among the client-side options in [Player Settings](Player-settings.md), alongside the audio and comfort controls already described there. In keeping with the rule that settings never confer an advantage, turning gore down changes only what a player sees, never the readability of danger or anything the server treats as authoritative, so a threat that one player can see is a threat every player can see.
