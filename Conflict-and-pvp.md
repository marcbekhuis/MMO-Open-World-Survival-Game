# Conflict and PvP

The open world is dangerous above all because of the people in it. Beyond the protection of a guarded city this is a full player-versus-player world: any player may raise a hand against any other, and the contest over the reopened frontier described in [Setting and Lore](Setting-and-lore.md) is fought as much between players as against the wild. This document sets out where that conflict is allowed, what restrains it, and how it connects to the larger machinery of loss, reputation, and conquest.

## A World of Open Conflict

By default the wilderness is lawless. There are no flags to raise and no consent to give: step beyond the guarded ground and another player can engage you, and you them. This is the deliberate texture of the frontier, where every stranger on the road is a question and travel itself is a risk taken with open eyes. The reward for accepting that risk is the frontier's wealth, the rare materials, the leyline-rich sites, and the uncontested ground that all sit furthest from safety, so that the most dangerous country is also the most worth reaching.

## Safe Cities and the Reach of the Guard

Order exists where power enforces it. The capital and spawn cities are held by NPC guards strong enough that no player or party can hope to fight them, and within a city's bounds aggression against another player is met with overwhelming, immediate force. These cities are true havens, the places to respawn, trade, craft, bank, train, and gather without fear, the fixed points a player can always fall back to. The protection does not end abruptly at the gate, either: the settled land immediately around a capital is patrolled and comparatively safe, giving new characters room to find their feet, and that safety thins with distance until, deep in the frontier, there is no guard at all and the only law is the one players bring with them.

## The Weight of a Killing

Lawless does not mean consequence-free. Where the guard's reach ends, the social memory of the [Reputation System](Reputation-system.md) takes over, and a player who kills indiscriminately, preys on the weak, or spills blood at the edge of a settlement earns a notoriety that follows them: bounties posted for their death, guards and hunters turned hostile, services refused, and a name that arrives in a town before they do. This is the law of the frontier, enforced not by walls but by the reactions of a world that remembers, and it gives rise to bounty hunters, caravan escorts, and protection bargains as readily as it breeds bandits. A killing is always possible; it is rarely free.

## From Ambush to Conquest

Open-world PvP is the individual layer of a larger contest. What begins as a roadside ambush scales up through the organized warfare of [Raids and Conquest](Raids.md), where groups assault settlements and player bases to seize resources, break rivals, or take ground, and territory won or lost there feeds back into the standing, alliances, and enmities the reputation system tracks. A [guild](Guilds-and-factions.md) that holds a stretch of frontier holds its trade and its dangers alike. Whatever is taken across all of this, from a single corpse to a sacked storehouse, is governed by the rules in [Death and Loss](Death-and-loss.md) and flows onward into the [economy](Economy.md).
