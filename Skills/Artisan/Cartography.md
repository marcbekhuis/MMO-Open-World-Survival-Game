In most games, maps are universally detailed and updated for all players as they explore. However, this game introduces a dynamic cartography skill, making exploration and map creation a specialized and valuable endeavor.
Overview of the Cartography System
Players start with a basic map system that shows only rudimentary details such as coastlines, major rivers, and rough terrain. To improve the level of detail displayed on their map, players must level their Cartography skill.
Leveling Cartography
Exploration:
Players earn a small amount of XP for the Cartography skill each time they discover new areas of the world. This encourages players to actively explore uncharted territories.
Re-exploration:
Once the Cartography skill levels up, players can revisit previously explored areas to unlock additional map details and gain a small amount of XP. However, this re-exploration provides only a limited amount of XP and is not sufficient to significantly progress the skill.
Crafting Map Items:
To advance Cartography effectively, players must craft map items. These items capture detailed information about the areas the player has explored and can be shared with other players.
Map Items
Purpose:
Map items allow cartographers to share specific area data with other players. These items will display detailed information for a limited area of the world, such as landmarks, dungeon entrances, or resource nodes.
Player Economy:
Crafted map items will be valuable commodities for players, especially those who rely on navigation to locate resources, avoid danger, or find hidden areas. Cartographers can sell or trade these items, creating an in-game economy around map creation.
Skill Progression
At Level 0, maps show only basic outlines of water bodies, city locations, and roads. No detailed features like terrain elevation, caves, or smaller landmarks are included.
As players level their Cartography skill, maps become progressively more detailed, adding features such as:
Terrain coloring (e.g., forests, mountains, and deserts).
Points of Interest (POIs) such as caves, ruins, and dungeons.
Resource locations for crafting materials.
Names of cities, regions, and landmarks.
At Max Level, the Cartography skill allows players to generate highly detailed, full-color maps that include all the information a player might need: named locations, dungeon entrances, resource nodes, and intricate terrain details.
Gameplay Implications
Player Collaboration:
Players who don’t invest in Cartography can benefit from purchasing map items crafted by skilled cartographers, fostering cooperation and trade between players.
Exploration Incentive:
The system rewards adventurers for venturing into uncharted areas. Skilled cartographers may even become guides for others, offering not just maps but also knowledge of the world’s most dangerous and rewarding locations.
Dynamic Role-Playing:
Cartographers have a unique role in the game, offering both utility and prestige. Master Cartographers might become famous within the player community for their comprehensive, accurate maps.
Example Use Case
A player is attempting to find a rare dungeon hidden deep in a dense forest. Without a detailed map, they would need to search blindly, risking danger and wasting time. However, a skilled cartographer has crafted a detailed map item marking the dungeon entrance, nearby hazards, and resource deposits. The adventurer purchases the map, saving time and gaining a strategic advantage.
This system not only enhances immersion but also adds a layer of strategy, economy, and individuality to the game world. Players will come to value not just what they discover but also the role they play in documenting and sharing the world’s secrets.
