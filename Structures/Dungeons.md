# Dungeons

Dungeons are ancient, indestructible superstructures that defy ordinary architecture. They may appear modest from the outside while opening into impossible interior spaces, deep halls, sealed vaults, and routes that do not map cleanly onto the surrounding terrain.

## Design Role

Dungeons provide concentrated challenge inside a game that otherwise emphasizes open-world travel. They should feel dangerous and rewarding without replacing the wilderness. Outdoor creatures may be larger or more disruptive, but dungeons create tighter pressure through traps, navigation, resource attrition, scripted encounters, and escalating depth.

## Progression

The deeper players venture into a dungeon, the more valuable the rewards should become. Early rooms can support solo scouting, lore discovery, and light combat. Lower levels should demand preparation, party roles, keys, environmental tools, or knowledge earned elsewhere in the world.

## Rewards

Dungeons can reward players with rare materials, spell clues, faction relics, crafting patterns, lore, and high-value treasure. The best rewards should pull players back into the wider world: a dungeon map points to a mountain shrine, a relic changes reputation with a settlement, or a crafting recipe requires materials from multiple biomes.
