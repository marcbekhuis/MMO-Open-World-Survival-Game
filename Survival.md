# Survival

Survival is the pressure that turns a walk through the wilderness into a series of decisions. The world wants players to prepare, to choose routes, and to weigh whether they can afford to push on, and it creates that pressure through hunger, fatigue, and the hazards of the country itself rather than through busywork. The guiding rule, set out among the design pillars in [README](README.md), is that survival exists to create decisions, not chores: a player should feel the cost of a long expedition without spending the game tending bars. This document covers what a character has to manage to stay alive, and how hard those pressures press depending on where they are and how far they have walked from safety.

## Survival as Decisions, Not Chores

The intensity of survival scales with distance from safety. Inside the ring of a capital, where a new character begins as described in [New Player Experience](New-player-experience.md), food and rest are trivially available and the pressures fade into the background, which is part of why a city merchant who never leaves the markets of [Economy and Trade](Economy.md) can play a full game without ever fighting the wilderness. Walk into the frontier and the same needs sharpen into real constraints: every meal carried has weight under the rules in [Inventory](Inventory.md), every night away from shelter costs something, and every biome adds a hazard of its own. The further a player commits, the more survival shapes what they can attempt, which is exactly the trade the design wants a player to feel when they decide how deep to go.

## Food and Hunger

Hunger is the steady clock of any expedition. A character who eats stays at full capability; one who lets hunger run drifts toward weakness, losing stamina recovery and eventually health, so food is the resource that ultimately limits how long a player can stay in the field. Rations differ in what they cost to carry and what they restore: foraged and raw food is heavy for what it gives and may carry its own risks, while cooked meals prepared through the survival and artisan skills of [Skills](Skills.md) and the recipes of [Crafting](Crafting.md) are lighter, more nourishing, and can grant short-lived benefits worth packing for. Because food takes up weight and space that compete with everything else a player wants to carry, provisioning a journey is one of the first real decisions survival forces.

## Fatigue and Rest

Fatigue is the second standing pressure, separate from the moment-to-moment stamina spent on sprinting and swinging a weapon. It accrues over a long stretch of activity without proper rest, and a fatigued character recovers stamina more slowly and grows less effective until they sleep. Rest is therefore tied to shelter and to the [Building System](Building-system.md): a bedroll thrown down in the open restores less and leaves a player exposed, while a secured camp or a bed inside a base gives a full, safe recovery. This makes the question of where to spend the night a genuine one, and it gives player-built footholds a survival value beyond storage and defence.

## Temperature and Exposure

The world's climates are a hazard in their own right, and temperature is the pressure that ties survival most directly to the biomes. The cold of the [Tundra](Biomes/Tundra.md) and the high passes of the [Mountain Range](Biomes/Mountain-range.md) drains a poorly dressed traveller and can kill outright in a storm, while the heat of the [Scorching Desert](Biomes/Scorching-desert.md) bakes by day and the same cloudless sky turns bitter after dark. Players answer exposure with the right clothing, with fire and shelter, and with timing, and the swings of [Weather, Time, and Seasons](Weather-time-and-seasons.md) decide how hard the pressure presses on any given crossing. Exposure is not a bar to be topped up so much as a condition to be managed by preparation, which is why packing for the climate matters as much as packing food.

## Wounds, Illness, and Recovery

Beyond the standing meters, the hazards of the world act on a character directly. Serious wounds from the brutal combat described in [Content and Tone](Content-and-tone.md) can linger as conditions that hamper a character until they are treated, so a hard fight survived can still leave a player limping home, and the bandages, salves, and remedies that mend them come from the survival and crafting trades. Particular biomes carry particular afflictions: the [Swamp](Biomes/Swamp.md), above all, breeds fevers and rot that punish the unprepared, turning illness into a regional hazard a careful traveller plans around. These are pressures the world applies rather than bars the player maintains, and surviving them is a matter of treatment and avoidance rather than constant upkeep.

## The Survival Skillset

Everything above rewards investment in the survival disciplines of [Skills](Skills.md): navigation that keeps a traveller from getting lost, campcraft that makes a night in the open safe, cooking that turns raw food into something worth carrying, weather resistance that blunts exposure, and tracking that turns the wilderness into a readable place. A character who has practised these skills can go further on less and weather conditions that would turn back the unprepared, so survival is not only a tax on play but a specialisation a player can lean into, the foundation of the self-sufficient frontier life set against the city merchant in [Player Progression](Player.md).
