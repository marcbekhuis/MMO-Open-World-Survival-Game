The magic system is divided into distinct elements: water, fire, earth, wind, light, and more. Players start with one basic spell unlocked from each element. These initial spells are relatively weak, but casting them earns experience points (XP), allowing players to level up their spells over time.
As a spell levels up, players can use points earned to enhance its attributes, such as size, speed, mana consumption, and cooldown. However, enhancing certain attributes comes with trade-offs—for example, increasing a spell’s size and power will also raise its mana cost and casting time. This system encourages players to carefully balance their upgrades to suit their playstyle.
Once a spell reaches a certain level, it can unlock new, more powerful spells. Some of these advanced spells may require multiple existing spells to reach specific levels, while others may combine elements from different schools of magic. This progression system encourages experimentation and strategic combinations, offering players deep customization and a sense of discovery.
