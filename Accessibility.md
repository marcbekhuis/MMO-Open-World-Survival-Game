# Accessibility

A demanding game does not have to be an exclusionary one. The frontier is meant to be hard because of the choices it forces and the players who contest it, never because a player cannot read the interface, hear a warning, or hold the controls comfortably. This document sets the commitment that the game's difficulty lives in its design rather than in barriers to perceiving or operating it, and it develops the accessibility promises made throughout [UI and HUD](UI-and-HUD.md) and [Player Settings](Player-settings.md).

## Seeing the Game

The interface is built to be read by as many players as possible. Text and HUD elements scale, important cues carry colourblind-safe shapes and contrast rather than leaning on colour alone, and the adaptive contrast described in [UI and HUD](UI-and-HUD.md) keeps information legible against everything from snowfields to blood-dark interiors. Players sensitive to intense imagery have the gore controls of [Content and Tone](Content-and-tone.md) together with options to temper flashing and harsh effects, so that the game's extremes of beauty and horror stay something a player tunes rather than simply endures.

## Hearing the Game

Because sound is genuine information in this world and not only atmosphere, as set out in [Audio Direction](Audio-direction.md), nothing critical is allowed to live in audio alone. Spoken content is subtitled, and the directional danger cues, creature tells, and weather warnings a careful player relies on are mirrored by visual or haptic alternatives, so a player who cannot hear an ambush coming is never simply at its mercy. The layered mix of [Player Settings](Player-settings.md) lets those cues be lifted above the music and ambience that would otherwise mask them.

## Operating the Game

Controls are fully remappable, and the interface supports the range of ways players actually play, from alternative input devices to options that ease timing-heavy or simultaneous inputs. This commitment is also what makes the eventual move to console controls noted in [UI and HUD](UI-and-HUD.md) a natural extension rather than a rebuild, since a control scheme designed to flex for accessibility is already designed to flex for a gamepad.

## Fairness Without Compromise

Accessibility options follow the same rule as every other setting: they change how a player perceives and operates the game, never the rules the game runs by. The aim is parity, giving every player the same access to the information and control the game assumes, so that an accessible setup is never a disadvantaged one and never an unfair edge either. A threat that any player must be able to react to is presented through more than one channel precisely so that meeting it stays a test of skill for everyone.
