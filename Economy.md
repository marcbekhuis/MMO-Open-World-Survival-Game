# Economy and Trade

The wealth of this world is made by its players. Almost everything of value, the gear that wins fights, the food that sustains an expedition, the walls that hold a base, begins as raw material pulled from the land and ends as the work of a crafter, and the economy is the web of trade that moves those goods from the hands that make them to the hands that need them. This document sets out how value is created, how it changes hands, and how the design keeps it scarce enough to matter.

## A Player-Made Economy

Production belongs to players. Gatherers work the resources of each biome, the crafters and artisans of the [Skills](Skills.md) system turn those resources into equipment, consumables, and structures, and the result is that the overwhelming majority of goods in circulation passed through a player's hands first. NPC vendors exist, but deliberately as a floor rather than a marketplace: they buy low and sell high, offering emergency liquidity and a price beneath which nothing quite falls, precisely so that trading with another player is almost always the better deal. The economy a player moves through is one other players built.

## Markets, Shops, and Trade Routes

Goods change hands through the player shops and stalls raised in settlements via the [Building System](Building-system.md), through the markets that grow up around capitals and trade hubs, and through deals struck directly on the road or in a guild hall. Because every item carries real weight under the rules in [Inventory](Inventory.md), moving goods is itself an act with cost and risk, and regions specialize: ore from the mountains, reagents from the swamps, and food from the plains each command different prices in different places, and the gap between them is a merchant's living. Carrying that value across open country makes traders and their caravans natural prey, which ties the economy directly to [Conflict and PvP](Conflict-and-pvp.md) and to the raiding of supply lines in [Raids and Conquest](Raids.md).

## Currency and the Fight Against Inflation

A persistent world generates wealth without pause, and unless losses balance the gains, currency and goods inflate until neither means anything. The economy is therefore built around strong sinks. Equipment wears and must be repaired, crafting consumes materials, settlements demand upkeep and can be taxed through the [Building System](Building-system.md), fast travel and portals charge their toll, bounties cost coin to post, and the full-loot deaths described in [Death and Loss](Death-and-loss.md) constantly destroy some goods even as they recirculate the rest. These drains are not friction to be minimized away; they are the mechanism that keeps a hard-won blade worth winning and a merchant's coin worth earning.

## Wealth, Risk, and Why Gear Matters

Because so much can be lost, everything carries a price. A full-loot world turns even the simplest decisions economic, what to wear into the frontier, what to leave banked, whether a single rare drop is worth a life's worth of risk, and a brisk secondary market grows out of the gear that changes hands at the point of a blade. None of this is for sale from outside the game: in keeping with the project's direction the economy stays free of pay-to-win pressure, with no power, currency, or advantage available for real money, so that wealth always traces back to what a player gathered, made, traded, or took.
